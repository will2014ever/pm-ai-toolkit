Executive Summary

AI is already part of daily PM work. The focus now is where it saves the most time.

1

Adoption is Real

PMs are already using AI in daily work. The focus now is where it saves the most time. Start small, focus on real pain points.

2

Need is Practical

The biggest value is in routine PM tasks. Meeting notes, updates, drafting, and synthesis benefit most. Research, communication, and execution support are top priorities.

3

Barriers are Real

Accuracy, data security, and skill gap are the main brakes on broader adoption. Governance and trust-building are essential for scaling.

Recommendation: To elevate PM capability, build a virtual AI team — a "one-person company" model where AI agents support you across research, synthesis, communication, and execution.

PMI Framework Reference

All use cases map back to PMI's 5 Process Groups and 10 Knowledge Areas (PMBOK 7th Edition).

5 Process Groups

1

Initiating

Define project, authorization, stakeholders

  • Develop Project Charter
  • Identify Stakeholders
  • Define high-level scope & objectives
  • Assign Project Manager
  • Determine initial risks & assumptions
  • Secure project funding & approval
2

Planning

Scope, schedule, cost, quality, risks

  • Develop Project Management Plan
  • Plan Scope & Collect Requirements
  • Create WBS (Work Breakdown Structure)
  • Define Activities & Sequence them
  • Estimate Durations & Costs
  • Develop Schedule & Budget
  • Plan Quality, Resources, Communications
  • Plan Risk Management & Identify Risks
  • Plan Procurement & Stakeholder Engagement
3

Executing

Coordinate people, resources, comms

  • Direct & Manage Project Work
  • Manage Project Knowledge
  • Manage Quality Assurance
  • Acquire, Develop & Manage Team
  • Manage Communications
  • Conduct Procurements
  • Manage Stakeholder Engagement
  • Implement Risk Responses
4

Monitoring & Controlling

Track, review, regulate performance

  • Monitor & Control Project Work
  • Perform Integrated Change Control
  • Validate & Control Scope
  • Control Schedule & Costs
  • Control Quality
  • Monitor Communications & Risks
  • Control Procurements
  • Monitor Stakeholder Engagement
  • Earned Value Analysis (EVM)
5

Closing

Finalize activities, lessons learned

  • Close Project or Phase
  • Transfer Deliverables to Operations
  • Release Project Resources
  • Document Lessons Learned
  • Archive Project Documents
  • Obtain Final Acceptance & Sign-off
