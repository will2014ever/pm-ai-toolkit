Deep Dive 5

Voice-of-Customer Deep Research

Turn scattered public feedback into structured input for concept definition and early design trade-offs. The fastest way to frame customer pain points before defining a new product concept.

Lifecycle: Framing & Vision PMI: Scope, Stakeholder, Quality AI Agent: Gemini Deep Research

Real Example: Kodiak Project

The team reviewed feedback on prior products (Rinca and Florence) to surface pain points and expectations before defining the new Kodiak concept. Gemini Deep Research collected signals from Amazon reviews, Reddit threads, and tech forums, then clustered them into actionable design themes.

Recommended Workflow

Collect Signals

Pull reviews and comments from relevant public sources: Amazon product reviews, Reddit discussions, tech review sites, YouTube comments, forums. Cast a wide net — AI will filter the noise later.

Sources: Amazon, Reddit, tech blogs, YouTube, forums

Analyze Themes with AI

Use Gemini Deep Research to cluster pain points, unmet needs, and positive signals. AI identifies recurring themes that might take a human days of reading to surface. Look for patterns across both prior products and competitors.

Tool: Gemini Deep Research (broad web analysis)

Create Design Brief

Summarize top issues and expectations into a short decision document. Structure: top pain points, feature expectations, unmet needs, competitive gaps, and design implications. This becomes input for concept definition workshops.

Output: 2-3 page design brief with data-backed insights

Review as a Team

Validate what is signal versus noise before using it in concept work. Not all public feedback is representative of your target user. Cross-reference with internal survey data, support tickets, and sales team input.

Review: PM + Design + Marketing alignment session

Prompt Templates

Research public customer feedback for [product category] across Amazon reviews, Reddit, and major tech review sites. Focus on these specific products: - [Prior product 1] (our product) - [Prior product 2] (our product) - [Competitor 1], [Competitor 2], [Competitor 3] Analyze and produce: 1. Top 10 pain points (ranked by frequency) 2. Top 10 most-praised features 3. Unmet needs mentioned 3+ times 4. Feature gaps vs. competitors 5. Emerging expectations (things users wish existed) For each finding, include example quotes and sources.
Based on this customer feedback analysis: [paste pain-point research from step 2] Create a design implications brief: 1. Must-fix issues (pain points we must address) 2. Must-keep features (things users love about our product) 3. Opportunity areas (unmet needs we could uniquely solve) 4. Design trade-offs to discuss with the team 5. Competitive positioning recommendations Format as a 2-page brief for a concept definition workshop. Include confidence level (high/medium/low) for each insight.
Using this customer research data: [paste analysis] Create a 1-page executive summary for stakeholders: - Key finding (1 sentence) - Top 3 customer pain points with business impact - Top 3 opportunities for the new product - Recommended design direction - Risk: what happens if we ignore this feedback Tone: data-backed, concise, decision-oriented.
PM value: Faster discovery, better design framing, and stronger early stakeholder alignment. What used to be 2 weeks of manual research is now 2 days of AI-assisted synthesis.
Tip: Run VOC research early in Framing, not after Vision is set. The best design decisions happen when customer voice shapes the concept, not when it validates a predetermined direction.
